There's a version of building that's about shipping features and hitting metrics. And then there's the other kind — the kind where you're trying to make something real, something that would matter even if nobody clapped.

I've been thinking about this a lot lately. The pressure to move fast, to ship, to show numbers — it's real. But so is the feeling when you've built something that actually helps someone.

<span class="arabic">إِنَّمَا الْأَعْمَالُ بِالنِّيَّاتِ</span>

Actions are by intentions. It sounds simple. It isn't.

The Prophet ﷺ said this about worship, but I keep coming back to it when I'm writing code. What are you actually trying to do here? Not the ticket, not the sprint goal — the real thing underneath it.

Most of the time the honest answer is: I'm not sure. And I think that's okay, as long as you're willing to keep asking.

---

I'm not going to pretend I have a framework for this. But some things that help me:

- Build something that has no business model first. Just to prove you can make something useful.
- Work on problems where the person you're helping can't pay you. The feedback is purer.
- Stop when you're done, not when you run out of time.

The last one is harder than it sounds.
